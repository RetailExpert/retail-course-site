The Consultative Framework

  • Step 1 - Open-Ended Questions: "What brings you in today?" "How will you use this?" "What's been your biggest frustration with [category]?"
  • Step 2 - Listen for "Why": The real reason people buy is often beneath the surface. Listen for emotions, not just facts.
  • Step 3 - Solution Mapping: Connect features to emotional benefitsâ€”"This is lightweight, so you'll love carrying it everywhere!"

Pro Tip: Repeat and summarize what you've heard. This shows you're listening AND gives the customer a chance to correct or clarify. It builds massive trust.

Psychology-Based Selling

Dr. Robert Cialdini identified 6 universal principles of influence. Master these and you become exponentially more persuasiveâ€”ethically.

The 6 Weapons of Influence:

  • 1. Reciprocity: People feel obligated to give back when given something first
    • Free samples, personalized tips, going above and beyond
    • Example: "Let me show you a little trick with this..."
  • 2. Commitment & Consistency: People want to act consistently with their words and actions
    • Get small "yeses" to build towards the big "yes"
    • Example: "Would you like to try this on?" â†’ "Does it feel comfortable?" â†’ "Should we get you checked out?"
  • 3. Social Proof: People follow what others do
    • Share what's popular, bestsellers, customer favorites
    • Example: "This is our #1 seller this month!" or "You have great tasteâ€”others love this too"
  • 4. Authority: People defer to experts
    • Display credentials, expert picks, professional endorsements
    • Example: "As someone who's worked with this brand for 3 years, I can tell you..."
  • 5. Liking: We say yes to people we like
    • Find genuine common ground, give compliments, mirror energy
    • Example: "I love that color too! Great choice"
  • 6. Scarcity: We want what's rare or running out
    • Honest scarcity creates urgency
    • Example: "This is our last one in this color!" or "Sale ends today"
