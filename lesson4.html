  • POS (Point of Sale) Zones: Should be visible, uncluttered, and have impulse items (gum, batteries, mini-products) nearby.
  • Price Callouts: Big, bold, contrasting labels. Use "was/now" tags for offers. Highlight value, not just price!

Mannequin Inspiration

  • Outfit Storytelling: Dress mannequins in full looks, including accessories. "Head-to-toe" increases multiple item sales.
  • Action Poses: Use dynamic or lifestyle poses (e.g., running, sitting, "in action") to inspire imagination.
  • Diversity: Use mannequins of different shapes, sizes, and skin tones for inclusivity and relatability.
  • Positioning: Place at entrances, power walls, or near new arrivals. Rotate outfits at least weekly.

5Color, Lighting, and Sensory Secrets

  • Color Psychology: Blue = trust; red = urgency; green = freshness; yellow = optimism. Use color to drive feelings and focus.
  • Lighting: Spotlight new or premium products. Warm lighting = inviting; cool lighting = modern/fresh. Use accent lighting on displays and mannequins.
  • Scents & Sounds: Scented zones and curated playlists can increase time in-store by 18%.
üåà Fun Fact: Stores with music matching their brand (tempo, genre) see a 9% higher average spend!

6Interactive, Seasonal & Social Displays

  • Interactive Displays: Testers, touchable products, digital screens, or "spin to win" games boost engagement.
  • Seasonal Themes: Rotate displays for holidays, local events, weather. Props make stories pop!
  • Instagrammable Moments: Create displays so fun or beautiful people want to take photos‚Äîfree marketing!
üí™ Challenge: Design a mini "photo spot" in your store. Encourage customers to share on social for a small reward!
üéØ Pro Tip: Change something in your visual merchandising every week, no matter how small. Shoppers notice freshness subconsciously.
